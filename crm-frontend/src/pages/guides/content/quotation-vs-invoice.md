# Quotation vs Invoice

Many teams confuse quotations and invoices, especially when they are moving quickly. The difference is simple once you separate the sales stage from the billing stage.

## What is a quotation?

A quotation is a proposal. It tells the customer:

- what you plan to provide
- how much it will cost
- how long the offer is valid

It is usually sent before the work starts or before the customer has confirmed the order.

## What is an invoice?

An invoice is a payment request. It is issued after the customer has accepted the price or after the goods or services are ready to be billed.

An invoice tells the customer:

- what they are being charged for
- how much they owe
- when payment is due

## When to use a quotation

Use a quotation when:

- you are proposing work
- the price needs customer approval
- you want a formal record before billing

## When to use an invoice

Use an invoice when:

- the customer has approved the quotation
- the work has been delivered or agreed
- you are ready to request payment

## Why the distinction matters

Keeping quotations and invoices separate helps your team:

- avoid billing too early
- track document status clearly
- keep a cleaner sales workflow
- convert approved quotations into invoices without retyping details

## A simple rule

Think of it this way:

- quotation = offer
- invoice = payment request

## Conclusion

If your team uses the right document at the right stage, customers get a clearer experience and internal follow-up becomes much easier.
