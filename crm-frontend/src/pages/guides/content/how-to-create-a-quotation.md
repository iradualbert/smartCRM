# How to Create a Quotation

Creating a quotation should be quick, clear, and easy for your customer to understand. A good quotation shows what you are offering, how much it costs, and how long the offer remains valid.

## Step 1: Start a new quotation

Open the quotations section and create a new quotation. Select the right organization first if you work across more than one business workspace.

## Step 2: Choose the customer

Select an existing customer or create a new one before continuing. Make sure the contact details are accurate, especially the name and email address you plan to use later.

## Step 3: Add the quotation details

Fill in the quotation date, validity period, and status. Most teams start with a draft so they can review the document before sending it.

## Step 4: Add your line items

Add the products or services you are quoting for. Each line should clearly show:

- item name
- quantity
- unit price
- tax if applicable
- line total

If you already maintain a product catalogue, reuse it to save time and keep pricing consistent.

## Step 5: Review totals and tax

Before saving, check the subtotal, tax details, and final total. This is the part your customer will scan first, so accuracy matters.

## Step 6: Pick a template

Choose a quotation template if your organization already has one. If not, the app can still fall back to a default professional template so you are not blocked.

## Step 7: Save and review

Save the quotation and open the detail page. Review the customer name, pricing, taxes, and PDF-ready output before sending it.

## Conclusion

A good quotation is not only about price. It is also about clarity, speed, and making it easy for the customer to say yes.
