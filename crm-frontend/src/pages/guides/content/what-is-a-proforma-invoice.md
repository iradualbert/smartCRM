# What Is a Proforma Invoice

A proforma invoice is a document sent before the final invoice. It looks similar to an invoice, but it is usually used for confirmation, customs, prepayment, or internal approval rather than immediate accounting settlement.

## What a proforma invoice is used for

Businesses often use a proforma invoice when they need to:

- confirm pricing before final billing
- request advance payment
- share expected charges for approval
- support import, export, or shipping paperwork

## What makes it different from an invoice

A standard invoice is the formal payment document used for billing and collections. A proforma invoice is usually informational or pre-billing in nature.

In practical terms:

- proforma invoice = expected charges before final billing
- invoice = final payment request

## When to create one

Create a proforma invoice when:

- the customer needs a formal cost document before you issue the invoice
- your process requires deposit or prepayment handling
- the shipment or service needs a pre-billing document first

## What to include

A clear proforma invoice should include:

- customer details
- item or service details
- quantities and prices
- tax details where relevant
- total expected amount
- reference number and date

## How it fits into the workflow

In many teams, the flow looks like this:

1. create quotation
2. customer agrees
3. issue proforma invoice if needed
4. issue final invoice
5. record payment with a receipt

## Conclusion

A proforma invoice helps you move the deal forward before final billing. It is especially useful when approvals, deposits, or operational paperwork come before the final invoice.
