# How to Send a Quotation by Email

Once your quotation is ready, sending it by email should feel like part of the same workflow, not a separate task. The goal is to send a clean message with the right PDF attached and the right sender account selected.

## Step 1: Open the quotation email page

Go to the quotation you want to send and open the email action. The app will prepare the attachment for you if the PDF has not been generated yet.

## Step 2: Confirm the recipient

Check the customer email address before sending. If the customer record is missing an email address, update it first so the message goes to the right contact.

## Step 3: Choose the sender account

If your organization has sender accounts configured, choose the one that matches the team or mailbox you want the customer to reply to. If your workspace uses the default sending path, the app can still send without forcing extra setup.

## Step 4: Review the subject and body

Keep the subject clear and professional. In most cases, include the quotation number and customer name. The email body should explain what the quotation is and what the customer should do next.

## Step 5: Preview the attachment

Preview the quotation attachment before sending. This is a quick way to confirm:

- layout looks correct
- customer details are right
- totals and tax are correct
- filename looks professional

## Step 6: Send the email

When everything looks right, send the message. The app records the send event in the document activity so your team can see what happened and when.

## Step 7: Follow up if needed

After sending, monitor the quotation status and document activity. If the customer needs changes, update the quotation and send the revised version from the same record.

## Conclusion

Sending by email is most effective when the document, attachment, and message all stay connected in one workflow. That keeps follow-up easier for your team.
