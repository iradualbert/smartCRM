# How to Create an Invoice from a Quotation

One of the easiest ways to save time is to convert an approved quotation into an invoice instead of rebuilding the same document from scratch.

## Step 1: Start from the quotation

Open the quotation that has already been reviewed and accepted. Make sure the customer, line items, prices, and tax details are correct before converting it.

## Step 2: Use the convert action

Choose the action that creates an invoice from the quotation. The app carries the main document details forward so your team does not need to re-enter the same information.

## Step 3: Review the new invoice

After conversion, open the invoice and check:

- customer details
- invoice date
- payment terms
- due date
- line items and totals

## Step 4: Update invoice-specific fields

An invoice is not exactly the same as a quotation. Review any fields that are specific to billing, such as:

- due date
- invoice status
- final invoice number
- internal notes if needed

## Step 5: Generate the PDF

Generate or review the invoice PDF so you can confirm the final layout, totals, and filename before sending it to the customer.

## Step 6: Send the invoice

Use the invoice email flow to send the document with the correct attachment and sender account. This keeps the document history and email history connected.

## Step 7: Track payment and follow-up

After sending, monitor the invoice status. When payment is received, record it with a receipt so the workflow stays complete.

## Conclusion

Converting a quotation into an invoice keeps your process faster and more reliable. It also helps your team avoid copy-and-paste errors.
